# Bronx Nourish Access Cultural Icon Direction

Purpose: identify culturally resonant, memorable, shareable app icon directions for a Bronx / NYC / African American community food-help PWA.

## Selection Criteria

- Must feel local, warm, dignified, and useful.
- Must be readable at phone home-screen size.
- Must not imply official government status.
- Must not rely on protected trademarks, borough seals, MTA marks, sports logos, or copied cultural artwork.
- Must be simple enough to become a memorable campaign symbol.
- Must support the behavior: save to phone, share with family, share with neighbors, share in groups.

## Strong Cultural Icon Concepts

### 1. The Bronx Bodega Bag
A simple grocery bag icon with a heart/leaf cutout and bold Bronx-green background.

Why it works: bodegas are highly recognizable neighborhood food-access anchors. A bag means practical help, not charity.

### 2. The Stoops + Sun Icon
A warm stoop silhouette with a rising gold sun and small food/leaf mark.

Why it works: stoops represent neighborhood connection, conversation, trust, and word-of-mouth.

### 3. The Boogie Down Basket
A food basket with subtle rhythmic motion lines and a gold accent.

Why it works: references Bronx cultural energy without copying music symbols or protected marks.

### 4. The Community Hand + Apple
A strong hand holding an apple/leaf in a circular badge.

Why it works: support, dignity, nourishment, and care. Good for African American community resonance when drawn respectfully and simply.

### 5. The Bronx Heart-Apple
A heart-shaped apple inside a green circle with a small gold shine.

Why it works: emotional, easy to remember, friendly, family-safe.

### 6. The Corner Store Light
A simple lit storefront awning shape with a leaf/bag symbol.

Why it works: evokes neighborhood familiarity and safety.

### 7. The Helping Hands Circle
Two abstract hands forming a circle around a leaf or apple.

Why it works: community care, mutual aid, and shareability.

### 8. The Gold Crown Leaf
A leaf shaped like a subtle crown.

Why it works: pride, dignity, royalty, empowerment. Must be used carefully so it does not feel flashy or unrelated to food help.

### 9. The MetroCard-Inspired Help Card
A simple yellow/gold resource card with a green food-help mark.

Why it works: NYC memory cue. Avoid copying the actual MetroCard design or MTA trade dress.

### 10. The Block Party Table
A simple table with apple/leaf and community circle.

Why it works: food, sharing, togetherness, local joy.

## Recommended Winner

The Bronx Bodega Bag.

Reason: It is local, instantly understandable, food-related, respectful, non-political, not government-implying, and easy to make viral as a saved/shareable icon.

## Recommended Visual Formula

- Background: deep Bronx green
- Main symbol: cream grocery bag
- Cutout: gold heart-leaf or apple-heart
- Small mark: BNA or Bronx Nourish Access initials only if readable
- Shape: rounded app icon with strong contrast

## Share CTA Pairing

Visible button: Share This

Supporting line: Help another Bronx household check available food help options.

Tone: helpful, neighbor-to-neighbor, not guilt-based.

## Avoid

- Statue of Liberty if the goal is Bronx-specific warmth
- NYC skyline if too generic
- MTA logo or MetroCard copy
- Yankees/Mets/NBA/NFL references
- raised fist unless the campaign is explicitly justice/advocacy focused
- government-looking seals
- complex illustrations that fail at app icon size
